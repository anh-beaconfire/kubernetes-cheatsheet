Reliability Best Practices

Multiple replicas

Run at least 2-3 replicas to avoid single-pod outages and ensure rolling updates do not cause downtime.

Topology spread constraints

Distribute Pods across nodes/zones to avoid correlated failures and improve resilience to node or AZ loss.

Pod anti-affinity

Ensure critical Pods do not land on the same node; reduces risk of losing multiple replicas at once.

PDBs

Define how many Pods must remain available during disruptions to prevent outages during node drains or upgrades.

Autoscaling

Use HPA/VPA to handle traffic spikes and resource variability; prevents overload and improves service stability.

Observability

Collect metrics, logs, and traces to detect issues early and understand system behavior under load.

Alerting

Set actionable alerts on SLOs, latency, errors, and saturation to catch reliability issues before users notice.

Graceful shutdown

Handle SIGTERM properly and honor terminationGracePeriodSeconds to avoid dropped requests during rollouts.
