Scaling

Scheduling: NodeSelector, Affinity, Taints, Tolerations

  • nodeSelector: simplest form of scheduling — exact match on node labels.
  • nodeAffinity: advanced rules; supports required (hard) and preferred (soft) placement.
  • podAffinity / podAntiAffinity: co‑locate Pods together or spread them apart across nodes/zones.
  • taints: repel Pods from nodes unless they explicitly tolerate the taint.
  • tolerations: allow Pods to be scheduled onto tainted nodes.
  • Use labels consistently — they are the foundation of all scheduling rules.
  • Avoid over‑constraining scheduling — too many rules lead to Pending Pods.
# nodeSelector: simple, exact match
nodeSelector:
  workload: gpu

---

# nodeAffinity: required (hard rule)
affinity:
  nodeAffinity:
    requiredDuringSchedulingIgnoredDuringExecution:
      nodeSelectorTerms:
        - matchExpressions:
            - key: node-type
              operator: In
              values: ["high-memory"]

---

# nodeAffinity: preferred (soft rule)
affinity:
  nodeAffinity:
    preferredDuringSchedulingIgnoredDuringExecution:
      - weight: 50
        preference:
          matchExpressions:
            - key: zone
              operator: In
              values: ["us-east-1a"]

---

# podAntiAffinity: spread Pods across nodes
affinity:
  podAntiAffinity:
    requiredDuringSchedulingIgnoredDuringExecution:
      - labelSelector:
          matchLabels:
            app: api
        topologyKey: "kubernetes.io/hostname"

---

# Tolerations: allow scheduling onto tainted nodes
tolerations:
  - key: "dedicated"
    operator: "Equal"
    value: "platform"
    effect: "NoSchedule"

---

# Example taint applied to a node (kubectl)
# kubectl taint nodes node1 dedicated=platform:NoSchedule
